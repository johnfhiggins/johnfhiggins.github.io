  • Week 2 (9/15 & 9/16), Preferences, Indifference Curves, and Utility Functions: Handout, Supplementary Handout, Solutions

    Additional resources:

    • EconGraphs - graphical example of indifference curves, an excellent visualization of indifference curves, utility functions, and marginal rate of substitution. You can drag the consumption bundle around and see the utility level, the indifference curve, and the marginal rate of substitution! You can also look at different utility functions and see how they are different.

    • EconGraphs - relationship between indifference curves and utility function. This page shows how the indifference curves are related to the utility function (in this case, the Cobb-Douglas utility function). Specifically, the indifference curve for utility level u is the set of all combinations of goods 1 and 2 for which u(x1, x2) = u! In 3D, this means we basically slice the utility function at a certain height and see the combinations of inputs which lie below that slice

  • Week 3 (9/22 & 9/22), Budgets, Optimal Choice, and Demand: Handout, Solutions

    Additional resources:

    • EconGraphs - graphical example of budget sets, it allows you to change prices and budget level to see how the budget line changes.

    • EconGraphs - consumer optimization, shows indifference curves and marginal utility. This shows how we can relate the MRS to the price ratio and consumer utility maximization.
